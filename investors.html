The primary route of infection is the inhalation of virus-laden respiratory aerosols, which initiate infection within the upper respiratory tract epithelium. This results in acute rhinitis (inflammation of the nasal mucosa), frequently accompanied by ocular irritation and reflex lacrimation (tearing) from stimulation of the lacrimal glands. As the virus multiplies and the infection progresses, downstream symptoms emerge, including mild pharyngitis (sore throat) and nausea, which may be accompanied by more significant gastrointestinal complications, i.e., diarrhea in severe cases.
