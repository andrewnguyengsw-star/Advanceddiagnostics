What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing uses qubits instead of classical bits. Through superposition and entanglement, quantum systems can process many possibilities at once, making them ideal for solving highly complex problems.

Graphic Idea: Classical bits (0/1) vs. Qubits (0 + 1 at once)

Understanding Complexity

Complexity measures how difficult something is to solve or predict.

In computer science: Resources (time, memory) needed to solve problems, expressed in Big-O notation.

In systems science: Complexity grows when many parts interact unpredictably (e.g., weather, biology).

Graphic Idea: Simple pendulum → Weather system (low → high complexity)

[Graphic Placeholder]

Why Complexity Matters in Quantum Computing

Classical computers struggle with exponential complexity (like simulating molecules).

Quantum computing can shift complexity boundaries, making the impossible solvable.

  • Shor’s Algorithm: Efficient factoring
  • Grover’s Algorithm: Accelerates searching
  • Quantum simulations: Model chemistry and biology

Tagline: Quantum doesn’t replace classical computing — it extends what’s possible.
