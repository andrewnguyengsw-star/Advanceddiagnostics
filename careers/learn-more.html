Gulf War Origins

Investigating the Origins

AECION wields hyper-advanced black technology capable of mapping and reconstructing Gulf War Syndrome at the level of individual neural impulses, revealing its full architecture with unprecedented precision and insight.

Gulf War Syndrome emerged after the 1990–1991 Gulf War, affecting returning veterans with chronic fatigue, cognitive issues, and other unexplained symptoms linked to chemical, environmental, and deployment-related exposures.

Gulf War Syndrome remains an unexplained illness, with multisystem symptoms and no single cause identified despite decades of research.

We will begin this today at 10:12 AM Central Time on October 20, 2025. Our goal is to demonstrate just how advanced our black technology truly is.

By reconstructing all neural impulses of Gulf War Syndrome, our research identifies the nostrils as the primary point of entry for the undiscovered catalyst.

Our neural mapping shows that inflammation in the nostrils and activation of the lacrimal gland mark the very first step in the onset of the illness. Whatever the agent was, it provoked mucous membrane irritation and respiratory distress.

After translating all deviations in the peripheral and central nervous systems, we observe a shift in lymphatic activity accompanied by a rise in body temperature. The coordinated response of these nerve networks to the agent culminates in what we recognize as the cardinal signs of inflammation, or calor, revealing the systemic orchestration underlying the illness.
