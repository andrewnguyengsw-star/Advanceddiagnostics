Our Quantum Application

Observing biology in real time at the quantum level — from molecules to entire human systems.

1. The Challenge of Scale: Capturing the Anti-Jo-1 Antigen

Our quantum platform tackles one of the hardest problems in biomedicine: understanding the histidyl-tRNA synthetase (HisRS) protein — the target of Anti-Jo-1 antibodies.

  • Massive Complexity: HisRS contains 6,000–11,000 atoms, making it too complex for classical simulation.
  • Beyond Classical Simulation: Accurately modeling folding, interactions, and dynamics requires solving the quantum behavior of every electron and nucleus — beyond even the fastest supercomputers.
  • Beyond Current Quantum Limits: NISQ-era quantum computers can only handle very small molecules. Simulating HisRS at biological scale would normally require millions of error-corrected qubits.

Our application overcomes this by using quantum sensing and entanglement rather than brute-force simulation — extracting real-world, real-time data directly from the biological system.

2. The Method of Observation: Quantum Sensing of Disease in Action

Instead of simulating molecules in silico, our approach observes biology as it happens — turning living molecular systems into quantum-readable data sources.

Quantum Sensing Network

A distributed array of quantum probes interfaces with the biological environment containing HisRS.

Entangled Measurement

Probes are quantum-entangled, so a perturbation at one point in the protein is correlated across the network, providing a synchronized view of molecular dynamics.

Ultra-Sensitive Coherence Tracking

Each probe’s quantum state is exquisitely sensitive to local electromagnetic fluctuations caused by protein folding, domain motions, and molecular interactions.

4D Pathology Mapping

By measuring changes in probe coherence, we reconstruct a spatiotemporal map of HisRS behavior — watching in real time how misfolding triggers the autoimmune cascade of antisynthetase syndrome.
