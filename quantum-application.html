Our Quantum Application

Observing biology in real time at the quantum level — from molecules to entire human systems.

The Challenge of Scale

Understanding the histidyl-tRNA synthetase (HisRS) protein, the target of Anti-Jo-1 antibodies, is one of biomedicine’s hardest problems.

  • Massive Complexity: HisRS has 6,000–11,000 atoms — too large for classical simulation.
  • Beyond Classical Simulation: Modeling folding, interactions, and dynamics requires full quantum behavior — beyond supercomputers.
  • Beyond Current Quantum Limits: NISQ devices handle only small molecules; large-scale simulation would require millions of error-corrected qubits.

We bypass brute-force simulation by using quantum sensing and entanglement to extract real-world, real-time biological data.

Quantum Sensing in Action

Instead of simulating molecules, we observe biology as it happens — turning molecular systems into quantum-readable data.

Quantum Sensing Network

A distributed array of quantum probes interfaces with the biological environment containing HisRS.

Entangled Measurement

Probes are quantum-entangled, so a perturbation at one point correlates across the network, providing synchronized molecular insights.

Ultra-Sensitive Coherence

Each probe detects minute electromagnetic fluctuations caused by folding, domain motions, and interactions.

4D Pathology Mapping

Changes in probe coherence reconstruct a spatiotemporal map of HisRS behavior, revealing autoimmune cascades in real time.

What This Enables

  • Quantum Simulation at Biological Scale: Understand complex molecules without impractical qubit counts.
  • Quantum Metrology for Medicine: Non-invasive, real-time molecular observation.
  • Direct Insight: Observe quantum-level events that initiate disease, enabling precise interventions.

Observing Disease Through Entanglement

  • Biological Trigger: Elevated Anti-Jo-1 antibodies bind HisRS, triggering tissue damage and inflammation.
  • Electrochemical Signaling: Peripheral and central nervous system signaling alters polarization gradients.
  • Quantum Coupling: Entangled photons detect these shifts and relay data to remote readouts.
  • Real-Time Observation: Captures collective molecular and electrochemical dynamics at disease-relevant concentrations.

Universal Quantum Biosensing

Our platform captures any human biological state — from single-molecule interactions to whole-body signaling — in real time.

  • Beyond Autoimmunity: Autoimmune diseases, cancer, neurodegeneration, infectious diseases, metabolic/hormonal regulation, drug-target interactions.
  • Mechanism: Quantum sensing network, signal coupling, entangled readout, spatiotemporal reconstruction.
  • Distinct Approach: Observes biology directly, bypassing massive fault-tolerant qubit requirements.

Comparison with Quantum Computing

Quantum Computing

  • Goal: Simulate molecules or run general-purpose algorithms.
  • Requires millions of error-corrected qubits for large proteins.
  • Produces theoretical predictions.
  • Mainly for future drug discovery simulations or optimization.
  • Years away from simulating full biological systems at scale.

Our Quantum Biosensing Platform

  • Goal: Observe and analyze every human condition and drug in real time.
  • Uses quantum probes and entangled photons — no large-scale qubits required.
  • Produces real-world measurements of immune, neural, and metabolic activity.
  • Analyzes molecules, pathways, and interventions dynamically.
  • Applicable to any disease, biological signal, or human condition.
